Matla Life Plan & Benefits

The Matla Life Plan offers comprehensive financial protection through three key benefits: the Matla Wills and Estate Benefit, the Matla Family Benefit, and the Matla Life Benefit. These benefits are designed to support families during difficult times, ensuring stability, peace of mind, and security.

The Matla Wills and Estate Benefit offers a free will drafting, collection, safe-keeping, and unlimited amendments at no cost. This benefit ensures that your estate is legally secure and prevents unnecessary delays and costs during estate administration.

  • Free will drafting and collection service.
  • Unlimited amendments at no extra cost.
  • Covers estate legal costs up to R282,568.
  • Reduces trauma and potential financial ruin for your family.

Capital Legacy Solutions (Pty) Ltd is an authorised financial services provider. The Legacy Protection Plan™ is underwritten by Guardrisk Life Ltd, a licensed life insurer.

The Matla Family Benefit provides a lump sum payout upon the passing of you or a loved one. This helps ease the financial burden of funeral expenses while offering flexibility and support for extended family members.

  • Provides a lump sum to cover funeral and related expenses.
  • Cover ranges from R10,000 to R150,000.
  • Includes spouse, children, and extended family members.
  • Designed for flexibility and immediate support during difficult times.

The Matla Life Benefit provides a lump sum payout upon the policyholder’s death to ensure your loved ones remain financially secure. It’s designed to protect your family’s future by covering ongoing financial obligations.

  • Basic life cover with no medical examination required.
  • Cover up to R1,500,000.
  • Ensures your family remains financially secure after your passing.
  • Provides peace of mind and easy accessibility.

When reading a client’s policy document to prepare a quotation, look for the following details:

  1. Type of Policy — Is it a life cover, retirement annuity, funeral policy, etc.?
  2. Policy Number — Identifies unique or duplicate policies (same number = one policy).
  3. Commencement Date — Helps determine if waiting periods are completed.
  4. People Covered — Include date of birth and cover amount per individual.
  5. Total Premium — Total monthly premium paid by the client.

When creating a quotation using RMA’s premium calculator:

  • Lives can be removed from the policy to adjust the total premium.
  • Premiums automatically recalculate based on the number of insured lives.
  • Training videos for quotation creation are available through RMA’s internal resources.

RMA uses standardised documents for managing policy applications and updates:

  1. Application Form — Used to collect client details for a new or replacement policy.
  2. Cancellation Form — Used to cancel previous policies on the client’s behalf.
    • Prevents double debits.
    • Ensures client policies move fully to RMA.
    • Ensures claim eligibility without waiting period repetition.
    • Improves affordability.
  3. Amendment Form — Used to modify existing policies (e.g., remove or add lives).

Additional processes include:

  • Re-dating and Replacement — Adjusting start dates or replacing old policies.
  • Clients can also visit insurer branches to collect documents directly.
  • Conference calls can be made while with the client to verify or obtain consent.

When managing cancellations, the following terms are important:

  • Cooling-off Period: Time frame during which a new policy can be cancelled (31 days new, 60 days replacement).
  • Grace Period: 31 days to pay overdue premiums before the policy lapses.
  • Lapse: Policy ends after 2 unpaid premiums (not necessarily consecutive).
  • NTU: “Not Taken Up” — policy never activated due to non-payment or client withdrawal.
  • Cap Cover: The maximum coverage limit based on the main member’s chosen amount.
